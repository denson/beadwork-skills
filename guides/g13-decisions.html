beadwork · decisions

Decisions, with the "why" kept.

A decision in beadwork is just a record with a shape you already understand. Nothing new to learn — and six months on, "why did we decide that?" has an answer.

Title — the decision itself ("Auth: Auth0 vs in-house").
Description — the question and the options on the table.
Comments — the back-and-forth, each one signed and dated.
Closing note — the verdict and the reasoning behind it.

An engineer's architecture call

"REST vs gRPC for the orders service." The options get weighed in comments, the call is made with its reasoning. Six months later a new engineer asks "why gRPC?" — and the original thread answers, trade-offs and all, instead of being lost to a Slack history.

The same shape carries other kinds of decisions just as well — a lawyer's privilege log (each redaction recorded with its reasoning) or a small business's expense choices at tax time ("MailerLite over ConvertKit, cheaper at our list size"). Worked versions of those are on the examples page.

It's a genuine way to keep consistent, dated records, and there's good evidence it does that well — but it won't make a record bulletproof if someone sets out to dispute it, and it isn't a legal guarantee. What it gives you is the steady answer to "why," in one place, with dates and authors intact.

bw (beadwork) is an open-source tool by jallum. This guide is part of a plain-language set for using it without writing code.
