Where your data lives.

Beadwork keeps its notes wherever you point your AI to put them. By default that's your own computer — nothing uploaded, no account. Four options, with honest costs:

Where it livesWho can see itCost
On your computerdefault Only you, on this machine Free · no account
Your private cloud Only you, across your devices ~$4/month
Your team's cloud You + the people you share with ~$4/person/month
A public repository ⚠ Anyone on the internet Free

Local-only is the headline. It's free, needs no account, and nothing ever leaves your machine — many people never need anything more. Because it needs no connection, it works on a plane or in a clinic, and the notes stay in the room. The one trade-off: if the laptop dies, the notes go with it, which is the reason to add backup.

⚠ Public means public. Choosing a public repository makes your tickets, comments, and decision history visible to anyone on the internet — including draft ideas, client details, or anything sensitive that lands in a note. Pick it only for genuinely public projects. If you're unsure, stay local-only: you can always add backup later, but making private notes public is hard to undo.

If privacy is the whole point — legal, medical, financial, or anything sensitive — local-only is the right default. Backup and sharing are choices you add on purpose, not the place to start.
