beadwork · your first session

Your first time with beadwork.

Here's what getting started actually looks like — for you. Your assistant does the setup and the writing; your part is to talk.

  1. You say you'd like to start keeping track of something — "let's use beadwork for this project."
  2. Your assistant gets it ready. You might okay a step or two — installing it, choosing where your notes live — and it handles the rest.
  3. From then on, you just talk. It writes down what's worth keeping and reads it back whenever you ask: "what did we decide?", "what's left?"

That's the whole first session. You never type a command, and there's nothing to configure by hand.

A few words you might hear

You can ignore every one of these — they're here only if you're curious what a word means. Your assistant deals with all of them.

git — a free, very reliable tool for keeping a dated history of changes. Your AI installs and runs it.
repository (or "repo") — the folder your notes live in. Your AI creates it.
commit — one saved change, stamped with a date. Your AI makes these as it writes.
branch — a separate lane; beadwork keeps its notes on their own lane so they never mix with your other files. Your AI manages it.
sync — copying your notes to and from the cloud, only if you chose a cloud option. Your AI runs it.
ticket — beadwork's name for a single item: a decision, a task, or a question, with its own notes and history.

bw (beadwork) is an open-source tool by jallum. This guide is part of a plain-language set for using it without writing code.
