beadwork · a guide for everyone

What it looks like in real work.

Beadwork gives your AI a durable place to keep the things that matter — decisions, context, coordination. Here's what that looks like across very different kinds of work, from a single week to across whole organizations.

Over a week

A data analyst chasing an anomaly

An investigation into a strange metric runs all week. Each day, the assistant notes what it checked, what came back, and the next thing to test. On day seven, it writes the stakeholder summary by reading its own trail — grounded in the actual investigation, not reconstructed from a fading chat.

The record becomes the report.

Over a year

A lawyer keeping a privilege log

Every redaction is logged as a decision: the rule, the reasoning, who made the call, the date. Nine months later, opposing counsel challenges one — the assistant pulls it up and produces the original determination, word for word, with the reasoning intact.

A consistent, dated record of every call.

Across engagements

A solo business owner at tax time

Each expense gets a line of reasoning when it's made — "MailerLite over ConvertKit; cheaper at our list size." At tax time, the assistant pulls the year's decisions and formats them for the accountant.

An auditable history, without separate accounting software.

Across organizations

A warehouse manager and an outside vendor

A logistics partner's AI posts pickup schedules to a ticket both sides share; the manager's AI confirms them or proposes alternatives. Two organizations coordinating on their own time — with a full record and no shared chat thread to maintain.

Coordination that survives, across a boundary.

Same tool, four shapes — a week, a year, across engagements, across organizations. The one that fits is whichever is closest to your work.

And the honest flip side: if your work doesn't accumulate decisions, context, or hand-offs worth keeping, beadwork won't add much. It earns its place where the "why" and the "what we figured out" need to outlive the conversation.

bw (beadwork) is an open-source tool by jallum. This guide is part of a plain-language set for using it without writing code.
