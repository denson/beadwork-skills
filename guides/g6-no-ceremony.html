beadwork · working solo

Keeping your own notes? Skip the ceremony.

If you're one person tracking your own decisions, tasks, and notes, beadwork is about as simple as it gets: you talk to your assistant, it writes things down, and they're there tomorrow. That's the whole loop — nothing to set up per note, no filing system to maintain.

Beadwork can also run a full team workflow — separate work areas, reviews, a sign-off before anything "lands." That machinery exists for when several assistants or several people are working at the same time and need to stay out of each other's way. Working on your own, you need none of it. Your assistant just writes to your notes and reads them back.

"Note that we're going with the October timeline." — written down.
"What's still open on the Henderson project?" — read back.
"Mark the deposit reminder done." — updated.

No step you have to remember, no place you have to file things, no workflow to learn. You talk; it keeps the record.

If you ever do bring in a team, the heavier workflow is there waiting — but it's something you turn on, not where you start. For your own work, the simplest path is the whole path.

bw (beadwork) is an open-source tool by jallum. This guide is part of a plain-language set for using it without writing code.
