beadwork · how you use it

You just talk to your AI.

You don't operate beadwork yourself. You talk to your AI assistant in plain language, and it runs beadwork for you. The only skill you need is the one you already have.

Say what you want in plain words — "note that we decided on the October timeline," or "what did we conclude about that vendor last spring?" Your assistant turns that into the right beadwork actions, runs them, and tells you what happened. You never see a command unless you go looking for one.

You

  • Talk in plain language
  • Decide what matters
  • Read back what your assistant tells you

Your AI

  • Turns your intent into beadwork
  • Runs it and keeps the record
  • Reports back in plain words

That division is the whole point: beadwork is built for an AI to operate. You bring the judgment and the words; it brings the bookkeeping that survives a long chat going stale.

Already comfortable in a terminal? Beadwork is a normal command-line tool underneath, so you can drive it directly if you'd rather. You don't have to — and most people won't.

bw (beadwork) is an open-source tool by jallum. This guide is part of a plain-language set for using it without writing code.
