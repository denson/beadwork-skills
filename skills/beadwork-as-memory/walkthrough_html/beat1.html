beadwork-as-memory · Beat 1 · The hook

A few specific things have to survive.

Most of what your AI needs across sessions isn't the whole chat history. It's a few specific things: the decision you reached, the constraint you hit, the blocker you noted, the half-finished plan. Those are what need to outlive the session.

Memory isn't the chat. Memory is the few things you wrote down.

Chat-only AI assistants lose those things every time the session ends, the chat compacts, or the machine changes. bw (beadwork) gives your AI a place to write those few specific things down — durably, in your repo, in a format the next session can read.

The shape of the interaction doesn't change: you talk to your agent; your agent writes the durable parts to bw; tomorrow's session reads them.
