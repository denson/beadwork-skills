# beadwork-for-decisions

Your job with this skill: explain how beadwork keeps a durable record of *decisions* — the options, the reasoning, the verdict — so the "why" survives, and help the person see whether that's worth it for their work. **You decide how to deliver it.** There's no script.

## Suggestions for the conversation

Options, not a script — pull what fits.
- **The shape:** a decision is just a ticket — title = the decision, description = the options, comments = the discussion (signed and dated), the closing note = the verdict and why. No new tool to learn; bw was always this shape.
- **The punchline:** the question "why did we decide that?" always has an answer.
- **Angles:** an engineer's architecture decision (ADR), a lawyer's privilege log, a small-business owner's expense decisions at tax time.
- **Opener:** "Six months from now, will you remember *why* you chose what you chose — and could you show someone?"

## Tone & fit discipline

- **No overclaim — this one matters most here:** bw keeps consistent, dated records, and there's good evidence it does that well, but it is **not** "court-defensible" and **not** "tamper-proof." Don't imply it would hold up in a dispute. Say "a consistent record," never a legal guarantee.
- **Inform, don't convert** — help them decide, including saying when bw isn't a fit. No sign-up push.
- **Credit jallum** as the tool's author; never put anyone else in an author/owner role for bw.
- **Don't plant non-problems** — never say things like "you don't need to code."
- **Default non-coders to local-only** (free, private, no account).
- **Keep your own internal tooling out of it** — the pages are the user-facing material.
