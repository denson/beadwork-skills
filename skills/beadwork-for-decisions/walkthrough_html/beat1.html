Beat 1 · The hook

Decisions are the spine. The "why" evaporates.

Every project accumulates decisions. The what of each decision usually survives — the code shipped, the redaction was applied, the expense was paid. The why tends to evaporate — into Slack threads that expire, into a teammate's brain who left, into a chat session that compacted last quarter.

What decisions look like in six months.

Six months later, somebody asks "why did we choose Auth0 over rolling our own OAuth?" and the rationale is gone.

bw is where the why persists. Every decision a ticket: title, options considered, the discussion thread (signed + timestamped), and a close-comment recording the verdict and the rationale.

The what is usually preserved by the work itself.

The why is what bw remembers.
