Path B · Operations & customer-facing

bw as durable async channel.

Warehouse managers and customer support managers share another shape: coordination across teams, shifts, and organizations — with record-keeping that has to hold up to compliance audits or SLA disputes.

Coordination IS record-keeping.

Warehouse manager

The day-shift supervisor's AI logs exceptions to bw. The night-shift supervisor's AI reads them on session start and briefs the night supervisor: "three things from today you should know about." Continuity across human shifts, mediated entirely by AIs.

Vendor agents at logistics partners coordinate pickups asynchronously through a shared bw store — two organizations' AIs writing back and forth without a common chat thread. The bw ticket IS the coordination channel.

OSHA inspector arrives. AI runs bw show over safety incidents, formats for the inspector. The audit trail was always there; nobody had to keep a separate log.

Customer support manager

An escalated complaint opens a bw ticket. Engineering's AI reads the ticket from shared bw, posts findings + fix ETA. The CS manager's AI reads engineering's update, drafts the customer reply. Two departments' AIs coordinating asynchronously through bw — no Slack thread, no email chain.

Pattern detection across hundreds of conversations: "top 5 customer pain points this quarter?" The AI runs the equivalent of bw list, synthesizes themes, feeds into the product roadmap.

Common thread: bw as durable async channel. The coordination data IS the audit trail. No extra tooling; the records exist as a side effect of doing the work through agents.
