Beat 1 · The hook

AI assistants forget.

A whole industry is gearing up to sell you the answer. Look at bw — beadwork, by jallum — before you get locked in. It's free, open source, and might be all you need.

You've probably noticed: today's chat with your AI ends, and tomorrow's session starts blank. Open a second session of the same AI today and it doesn't know about the first either. You spend the first ten minutes of every fresh session re-explaining context you already explained yesterday.

Sessions end. Context shouldn't.

bw is the alternative that already exists. Three things it lets you do:

  1. Your AI remembers across sessions. Tomorrow's chat opens, the AI reads yesterday's notes, and picks up where you left off — no re-explaining.
  2. Multiple AI sessions coordinate as a team. Your design session, your implementation session, and your review session share one workspace and build on each other's work — you get a small team of specialist agents instead of one amnesiac generalist. Most often these are sessions of the same model (all Claude, all Gemini, etc.); the same mechanism also works across labs if you ever mix vendors.
  3. Your team of humans can share notes too. If you work with other people, everyone's AI assistants — and the humans themselves — can read and write to the same shared notes. One substrate that holds memory for one person, for a team of agents, or for a whole team of humans-plus-agents.

Three reassurances up front:

  • You don't have to learn the tool. Your AI does. bw ships with instructions that tell the AI how to install and use it; you mostly just authorize the steps.
  • It can stay entirely on your computer. Nothing has to be uploaded anywhere. Local-only is the default if you want maximum privacy.
  • It's free and open source. Works with any AI. No SaaS account needed for solo use; team coordination uses a regular GitHub repo.
