Path A · Technical roles

bw as audit substrate.

Engineers, data scientists, and logistics analysts share one thing: their work has to be correct and defensible — to senior reviewers, to stakeholders, to regulators, or to their future selves six months later.

Three roles, one shape.

Software engineer

A debugging session spans three days. Today's Claude reads yesterday's bw show and resumes — no re-explanation. On PR submission, three review agents (security, style, test-coverage) write structured verdicts to a shared ticket. A fourth "conflict-finder" agent reads all three verdicts and flags contradictions. The PR's audit trail is the bw history, signed and timestamped.

Data scientist

An investigation into a metric anomaly spans a week with daily comments. An analysis pipeline of three agents (data cleaner → statistical tester → visualizer) all write to bw. A methodology-auditor agent reviews the chain to catch gaps. Six months later, leadership asks "why did you conclude X?" — the answer is bw show, not "let me try to remember."

Logistics analyst

Vendor scorecard updates weekly. Same data feeds the operations team via shared bw — the analyst's AI writes the scorecard; the warehouse manager's AI reads it during pickup negotiations. Route-model iterations logged so abandoned approaches are explainable months later.

Common thread: bw as audit substrate. The history of why decisions were made — not just what was decided — is preserved automatically, by everyone working through their AI, from day one.
